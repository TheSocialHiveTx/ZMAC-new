24/7 Emergency

Emergency
Repair.

When a storm damages your roof and water is getting in, every hour matters. ZMAC Construction provides 24/7 emergency roof repair response throughout the Denver metro area — because storm damage doesn't wait for business hours, and neither do we.

Emergency roof repair after a storm involves two phases: immediate response to stop water intrusion and protect the property, followed by permanent repair once conditions allow. In the immediate response phase, ZMAC technicians install temporary protection — heavy-duty tarps, emergency patches, or temporary membrane — to stop active water intrusion and prevent further damage to the building interior. We document all damage thoroughly during this phase, which is critical for insurance claims.

The permanent repair phase follows once the weather has stabilized and the full extent of the damage has been assessed. ZMAC coordinates the permanent repair work with your insurance carrier, ensuring all storm-related damage is captured in the claim and that the repair scope is properly approved before work begins. We work efficiently to minimize the time your property is operating with temporary protection.

FAQ.

Emergency storm repair involves two phases. The immediate response phase includes: rapid assessment of the damage; installation of temporary protection (tarps, emergency patches, or temporary membrane) to stop active water intrusion; documentation of all damage with photos for insurance purposes; and securing any loose or displaced roofing materials that could cause additional damage. The permanent repair phase follows once conditions allow and includes all necessary repairs to restore the roof to full protection, performed with materials that match the existing system.

For active emergencies, we aim to have a technician on site within 2–4 hours of your call. Temporary protection can typically be installed in a single visit. Permanent repairs are scheduled as quickly as possible after the insurance claim is processed — typically within 1–3 weeks of claim approval depending on the scope of work and material availability.
