Fire Recovery

Fire
Damage.

Fire damage to a roof is one of the most serious situations a property owner can face. Whether the fire originated on the roof (from a lightning strike or ember exposure) or spread to the roof from inside the building, the damage assessment and restoration process requires specialized expertise. ZMAC Construction has the experience to assess fire-damaged roofing systems accurately and restore them safely.

Fire damage assessment is more complex than other types of roof damage because the visible damage often understates the true extent of the problem. Heat can compromise the structural integrity of roof decking and framing even when there's no visible charring. Smoke and heat can degrade roofing materials that appear intact. And water damage from firefighting efforts can be extensive, affecting insulation, decking, and interior finishes. ZMAC's assessment process addresses all of these factors to give you a complete picture of the damage.

We work closely with insurance adjusters and restoration contractors to ensure the roofing scope of work is properly documented and that all fire-related damage is captured in the claim. Our restoration work is performed to code, with all required permits, and we coordinate with other trades to ensure the roofing work integrates properly with the overall restoration project.

FAQ.

Fire damage roofing restoration involves: a comprehensive assessment of all fire, heat, smoke, and water damage to the roofing system; coordination with the insurance adjuster to document the full scope of damage; removal of all damaged roofing materials, decking, and framing as required; installation of new structural components to code; installation of new roofing system with all required underlayment, flashings, and penetration details; and final inspection and documentation. We pull all required permits and coordinate with the local building department throughout the project.

Fire damage restoration timelines vary significantly based on the extent of damage, the complexity of the structural repairs required, and the insurance claims process. Minor fire damage affecting only the roofing materials may be restored in 1–2 weeks. Major fire damage requiring structural repairs may take 4–8 weeks or more. We provide a detailed project schedule after the initial assessment and insurance scope is finalized.

Fire damage assessment goes beyond looking for ash. We examine your roof deck for 'alligatoring' of the wood, loss of structural load-bearing capacity in trusses, and thermal degradation of the roof membrane. Extreme heat can weaken even unburned roofing materials, making them brittle and prone to premature failure. Our HAAG-certified inspectors ensure no hidden heat damage is overlooked.
