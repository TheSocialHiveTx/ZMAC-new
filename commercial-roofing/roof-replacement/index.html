Full Replacement

Roof
Replacement.

When repairs are no longer cost-effective and your roof has reached the end of its serviceable life, ZMAC Construction delivers complete commercial roof replacement with minimal disruption to your business operations — from tearoff through final inspection.

Knowing when to replace rather than repair is one of the most important decisions a commercial property owner makes. If your roof is more than 15–20 years old, experiencing recurring leaks in multiple areas, showing widespread membrane deterioration, or has significant moisture in the insulation layer, replacement is almost always the more cost-effective long-term choice. Continuing to repair a failing roof system typically costs more over 5 years than a full replacement — and leaves you with ongoing disruption and liability exposure.

ZMAC's replacement process begins with a thorough assessment of your existing system, including infrared moisture scanning when appropriate, to determine the extent of damage and the best replacement approach. We can perform full tearoff and replacement, or in some cases install a new system over the existing one (recover) to reduce cost and waste. Every replacement project is managed from start to finish by our experienced project managers, ensuring the work is completed on schedule and to specification.

FAQ.

Commercial roof replacement involves removing the existing roofing system (or installing over it in a recover scenario), inspecting and repairing the roof deck as needed, installing new insulation, and installing the new roofing membrane or system. ZMAC handles all phases of this process, including disposal of the old materials, coordination with building occupants to minimize disruption, and final documentation for your records and insurance. We work with all major commercial roofing systems including TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen, built-up roofing, and metal.

Most commercial roof replacements take 1–2 weeks for buildings up to 30,000 square feet, depending on the system being installed and site conditions. Larger buildings or phased projects on occupied facilities may take longer. We provide a detailed project schedule before work begins, and we work to minimize disruption to your operations — including scheduling noisy tearoff work during off-hours when needed for sensitive occupancies.

Key signs include recurring leaks in multiple locations, widespread membrane cracking or blistering, water-saturated insulation, and a roof that has exceeded its 15-20 year design life. If repair costs exceed 30% of replacement value, or if you're dealing with structural concerns, a new roof is the safer and more cost-effective long-term investment.

While a 'recover' (installing over an old system) is possible if there's only one existing layer and the deck is dry, ZMAC often recommends a full tear-off. This allows us to inspect the structural deck for rot or corrosion and ensures that your new NDL warranty isn't compromised by old, failing substrate.

We specialize in working on occupied buildings. Our project managers coordinate logistics, debris management, and safety protocols to ensure your employees and customers remain safe. We often schedule high-noise tear-off work during off-hours to minimize impact on your operations.
