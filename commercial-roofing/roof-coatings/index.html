Coatings.

Commercial roof coatings are one of the most cost-effective ways to extend your roof's life, improve energy efficiency, and restore waterproofing protection — without the cost and disruption of full replacement. ZMAC Construction installs premium coating systems on commercial roofs throughout the Denver metro area.

Modern commercial roof coatings have advanced significantly in recent years. Today's silicone, acrylic, and polyurethane coating systems are engineered to withstand Colorado's extreme UV exposure, temperature swings, and hail events. A properly applied coating creates a seamless, fully adhered waterproof membrane over your existing roof surface — sealing seams, flashings, and penetrations that are the most common sources of leaks. Reflective coatings can also reduce rooftop temperatures by 50–80°F, significantly reducing cooling loads and energy costs.

Coatings are most effective when applied to roofs that are structurally sound but showing signs of aging — minor seam lifting, surface oxidation, or early-stage membrane degradation. ZMAC conducts a thorough assessment before recommending a coating system, including moisture scanning to ensure the existing insulation is dry. If wet insulation is present, it must be addressed before coating to prevent trapped moisture from causing further damage.

FAQ.

Commercial roof coating installation involves four phases: (1) thorough pressure washing of the existing membrane to remove dirt, debris, and biological growth; (2) repair of all seams, flashings, and penetrations with reinforcing fabric and sealant; (3) application of the base coat at the specified coverage rate; and (4) application of the finish coat to achieve the required dry film thickness. ZMAC works with leading coating systems including Conklin, Tremco, Henry, and Gaco Western, and selects the right system for your specific roof type and conditions.

Most commercial roof coating projects take 3–7 business days depending on roof size and weather conditions. Coating application requires dry weather and temperatures above 50°F, so we schedule projects carefully around Colorado's weather patterns. We provide a detailed project schedule before work begins and communicate any weather-related delays promptly.

For Colorado's flat commercial roofs, silicone is generally superior because it is inorganic and doesn't degrade under UV exposure or standing water. Acrylic coatings are excellent for sloped roofs or vertical surfaces where water sheds quickly and budget is the primary concern.

Most common systems—including TPO, EPDM, Modified Bitumen, and Metal—are excellent candidates for coating. The critical requirement is that the roof must be structurally sound and free of widespread subsurface moisture. We perform moisture scans on every project to ensure a successful bond.

White 'cool roof' coatings can reflect up to 88% of the sun's heat. By keeping your roof surface up to 50°F cooler, you can reduce your facility's cooling energy consumption by 15-30%, leading to significant operational savings during the summer months.

Shield
